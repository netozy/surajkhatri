    Intrapersonal Conflict

    It's within single person. When more then one similar type alternatives. Related with person personality, needs, values and norms. It raise due to goal conflict, frustation in work and lack of motivational unclear role. Intrapersonal conflict refers to a psychological struggle that may occurs within an individual conflicting thoughts, beliefs, needs, values & norms. This can result in feelings of discomfort, indecision as the individual tries to reconcile the conflicting aspects of themselves. Examples of intrapersonal conflict include deciding between two equally important goals, dealing with a difficult ethical dilemma, or struggling to defeating fear or insecurity.
    Intrapersonal conflict example could be person facing career decision. Intrapersonal conflict will also arise in personal relationships. Suppose, an individual may struggle to decide whether to deal with their hurtful behavior or to avoid conflict and maintain the relationship. Solving intrapersonal conflict requires self-reflection, and self-awareness. The individual may need to examine their thoughts, needs and goals to clarify and insight into their inner struggles. Additionally, seeking support from trusted professionals such as mentors and gurus, can help the person explore their options and develop strategies for managing the conflict.

    Interpersonal Conflict

    It is related with two different person of same organization. Personality clashes, goal disagreement, Communication failures are causes. Interpersonal conflict occurs with two or more individuals have differing opinions, thoughts, attitude, beliefs, or values that clash which create the situation like tension and misunderstandings. Interpersonal conflict can occur in various workplace, family, community due to communication breakdown, power struggles, or personality differences. Common cause of interpersonal conflict is breakdown communication. Suppose, two colleagues may have different opinions of a task, leading to confusion and frustration. In this case, the conflict can be solved through proper communication, clarifying expectations, expressing concerns, and seeking feedback. If the conflict occurs, it can escalate into a larger issues leading to ruin relationships and reduced productivity.

    Intergroup Conflict

    It is the conflict with the group of same organization. Formal and informal group conflict. It is a production depart and marketing depart conflict. It's occurs due to unclear structure communication failures, etc. Intergroup conflict can rise from factors such as historical grievances, economic disparities or competition for resources which can result negative outcomes, such as violence, discrimination, and social division. Example of intergroup conflict is ethnic conflict, which can rise from cultural, social or political differences between different ethnic groups. Example, the conflict between Hutus and Tutsis in Rwanda in the 1990s was by ethnic tensions, leading to violence.
    Promoting a culture of peace can help to reduce intergroup conflict. Educating individuals and communities about the dangers of violence and doing peaceful conflict resolution strategies like mediation, negotiation and collaboration. At last, intergroup conflict is a complex and challenging issues that can arise from a range of factors like cultural, social, political, and economic differences. Solving intergroup conflict requires a peaceful conflict resolution strategies like mediation, negotiation and collaboration. By working towards these solutions, it is possible to reduce intergroup conflict and promote a more peaceful society.

    Inter-organization Conflict

    It is the conflict between two different organizations which is related with politicies, etc. Common cause of inter-organizational conflict is competition. More than two organizations operate in the same market they may compete for customers, resources or market share due to which there will be inter-organizational conflict occurs such as price wars or efforts to sabotage competitors. For the reduction of inter-organizational conflict due to competition, organizations can establish clear boundaries and rules for competition. Next cause of inter-organizational conflict is resource scarcity. When resources such as funding, materials or personnel are limited organizations may compete for these resources which will lead to conflicts. Suppose, two non-profit organizations may compete for funding from the same donor which lead to tensions and conflict between organizations.
    To reduce inter-organizational conflict organizations can collaborate and share resources as well as engage in communication to prevent misunderstandings. Promoting a culture of cooperation and understanding can help to reduce inter-organizational conflict. This can involve promoting values such as trust, transparency, respect and encouraging organizations to work together. And finally, inter-organizational conflict is a experience that can arise from competition, resource scarcity or negotiation. Inter-organizational conflict requires effective communication, collaboration and finding common ground. By mutual understanding and respect, inter-organizational conflict can be solved which improved relationships and greater success.
